The math of mortality · 1-in-X ratios

Time between deaths
1/3 min
In the time it takes to read this dashboard, multiple lives have been lost on Indian roads. 172,890 deaths a year ÷ 525,600 minutes = 1 every 3 minutes.
Population at risk
1/8,100
Roughly 1 in every 8,100 Indians dies in a road crash each year - compared to 1 in 14,300 globally. India has 11% of world crash deaths but just 1% of world's vehicles.
Highway road network
5%→36%
National & State Highways together carry just 5% of India's road network but account for 60% of all road deaths. Highways need disproportionately more attention.
Working age victims
2/3
67% of all road fatalities are aged 18–45 - primary breadwinners and the productive backbone of the country. The economic cost to families is staggering.

Methodology: Collision-type accidents are estimated by multiplying the state's total accidents by national-level collision-type proportions (MoRTH 2023 Table 1.5). Persons killed in each collision type are estimated by applying that type's national killed-share to the state's total fatalities. MoRTH does not publish state-wise collision-type breakdowns directly.

Data & Methodology

Source files, data dictionary and how this dashboard was built.

Sources

  • MoRTH Road Accidents in India annual reports - 2019, 2020, 2021, 2022, 2023.
  • 2023 report is the master 5-year dataset (Tables 1.1, 1.4, 1.6, 5.1, 5.6, 6.1, 6.2, 6.3).

Coverage

  • National: 2005–2023 long-term trend; 2019–2023 sub-categories.
  • State / UT: all 28 states + 9 UTs (incl. Ladakh from 2020), 2019–2023.
  • City: 50 million-plus cities, 2022–2023 detail; top-10 trend 2019–2023.

Reconciliation

  • State-wise sums for 2019, 2022, 2023 reconcile to All-India totals exactly.
  • City-wise sums match the 50-city total exactly for both 2022 and 2023.

Deploy to Vercel + GoDaddy

  1. Drag this folder into vercel.com → auto-deploys.
  2. Buy a domain (e.g. crashinsights.in) on GoDaddy.
  3. In Vercel → Project → Domains, add the domain.
  4. In GoDaddy DNS, add A record (@ → 76.76.21.21) and CNAME (www → cname.vercel-dns.com).
  5. SSL is automatic. Live in 30-60 minutes.

Why this dashboard?

This India Road Crash Intelligence Platform brings together everything I've worked on for the past several years - geospatial analysis, crash data, blackspot identification, iRAP star ratings, data visualisation and urban planning. Two of my published research papers (15th and 17th Urban Mobility India Conferences) focus on pedestrian crashes in Chennai - a topic this dashboard quantifies at national, state and city level. Built as a public-facing tool to make MoRTH's annual report tables explorable, comparable and useful for policy-makers, researchers and citizens.
